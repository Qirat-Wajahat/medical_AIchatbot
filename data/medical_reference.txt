MEDICAL REFERENCE GUIDE
========================

COMMON COLD
-----------
Description: A viral infection of the upper respiratory tract, typically caused by rhinoviruses.
Duration: Usually 7-10 days
Prevention: Frequent hand washing, avoiding close contact with infected individuals
When to see a doctor: If symptoms persist beyond 10 days, high fever develops, or breathing difficulties occur.

INFLUENZA (FLU)
---------------
Description: A contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses affecting the nose, throat, and lungs.
Duration: 1-2 weeks
Prevention: Annual flu vaccination, good hygiene practices
When to see a doctor: Difficulty breathing, chest pain, severe weakness, high fever lasting more than 3 days.

MIGRAINE
--------
Description: A neurological condition characterized by intense, debilitating headaches often accompanied by other symptoms.
Duration: 4-72 hours if untreated
Prevention: Identify and avoid triggers, maintain regular sleep schedule, manage stress
When to see a doctor: First severe headache, sudden onset headache, headache with neurological symptoms.

GASTROENTERITIS
---------------
Description: Inflammation of the digestive tract, commonly called "stomach flu," caused by viruses, bacteria, or parasites.
Duration: 1-10 days depending on cause
Prevention: Hand hygiene, safe food handling, clean water
When to see a doctor: Signs of severe dehydration, blood in stool, high fever, symptoms lasting more than 3 days.

ALLERGIC RHINITIS
-----------------
Description: Allergic response to airborne substances like pollen, dust mites, or pet dander causing nasal inflammation.
Duration: Seasonal or year-round depending on allergen
Prevention: Avoid known allergens, use air filters, keep windows closed during high pollen seasons
When to see a doctor: Symptoms interfering with daily life, ineffective over-the-counter medications.

BRONCHITIS
----------
Description: Inflammation of the bronchial tubes that carry air to and from the lungs, often following a cold or flu.
Duration: Acute: 3-10 days; Chronic: 3+ months
Prevention: Avoid smoking, get vaccinated, practice good hygiene
When to see a doctor: Cough lasting more than 3 weeks, wheezing, high fever, blood in mucus.

URINARY TRACT INFECTION (UTI)
------------------------------
Description: Bacterial infection affecting any part of the urinary system including kidneys, bladder, ureters, or urethra.
Duration: 3-7 days with treatment
Prevention: Stay hydrated, urinate after intercourse, wipe front to back, avoid irritating feminine products
When to see a doctor: Pain, fever, back pain (possible kidney infection), blood in urine.

HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE)
-----------------------------------
Description: A chronic condition where blood pressure in arteries is consistently elevated, increasing heart disease risk.
Duration: Chronic condition requiring long-term management
Prevention: Healthy diet (low sodium), regular exercise, maintain healthy weight, limit alcohol
When to see a doctor: Regular monitoring required, immediately if severe headache, chest pain, or vision changes occur.

TYPE 2 DIABETES
---------------
Description: A chronic condition affecting how the body processes blood sugar (glucose), characterized by insulin resistance.
Duration: Chronic condition requiring lifelong management
Prevention: Maintain healthy weight, regular physical activity, balanced diet, limit refined carbohydrates
When to see a doctor: Regular monitoring required, immediately if extreme thirst, frequent infections, slow-healing wounds.

ANXIETY DISORDER
----------------
Description: A mental health condition characterized by excessive worry, fear, or nervousness affecting daily functioning.
Duration: Can be chronic if untreated; varies with treatment
Prevention: Stress management, regular exercise, adequate sleep, limit caffeine and alcohol
When to see a doctor: Symptoms interfering with work/relationships, panic attacks, thoughts of self-harm.

GENERAL MEDICAL ADVICE
-----------------------
- Always complete prescribed antibiotic courses
- Stay hydrated, especially during illness
- Get adequate rest to support immune system
- Maintain regular exercise routine when healthy
- Follow a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables
- Practice good hand hygiene
- Stay up to date with vaccinations
- Manage stress through healthy coping mechanisms
- Avoid self-medication without professional guidance
- Keep regular check-ups with healthcare provider

EMERGENCY WARNING SIGNS
------------------------
Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Sudden severe headache
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe abdominal pain
- Sudden weakness or numbness
- Confusion or difficulty speaking
- High fever with stiff neck
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Uncontrolled bleeding
